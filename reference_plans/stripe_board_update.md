# Stripe — Q4 board update
Audience: Stripe board + exec leadership

Preferences:
- Tone: executive, candid, growth-honest

## Opening thesis
- Payments volume growth re-accelerated in Q4 — we are back to compounding, not coasting.

## Q4 at scale
- $348B TPV processed in Q4, +29% YoY
- 142M active payment methods on file, +18% YoY
- 47 new countries live for Link, now 94 total

## Segment performance
- Enterprise: +34% YoY TPV — strongest quarter since 2022
- SMB: +21% YoY TPV — steady, slightly below plan
- Platforms (Connect): +41% YoY TPV — continues to be the growth engine [[highlight this in accent — it's the headline]]
- Subscriptions (Billing): +26% YoY ARR

## What worked in Q4
- Link conversion lift: +140bps on checkout conversion across enabled merchants
- Enterprise renewals: 97% gross retention, 114% net retention
- Radar ML models cut fraud losses 23% QoQ on the top 500 accounts
- Closed 11 of the top 50 target logos — beat plan of 8

## What didn't
- SMB new-logo acquisition missed plan by 12% — CAC up, conversion from free tier softer
- LatAm expansion slower than committed — Brazil launch slipped one quarter
- Terminal hardware margins compressed 4pts on component costs
- Two enterprise deals pushed to Q1 ($80M combined TPV commitment)

## Q1 risks we're watching
- Interchange regulation in EU — final rule expected Feb, could compress take rate 3-5bps
- FX headwind from strong USD — ~1.5% drag on reported revenue if spot holds
- Concentration: top 10 merchants now 28% of TPV, up from 24% a year ago
- Hiring pace in AI/ML below target — 62% of open reqs filled vs 80% goal

## What the board should take away
- Q4 proved the growth playbook still compounds; Q1 is about defending take rate and closing the SMB gap.
